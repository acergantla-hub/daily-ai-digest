### Amazon Plans to Sell Trainium AI Chips, Challenging Nvidia Directly

![AWS data center](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/aws-datacenter-1152x648-1754500479.jpg)

**Industry** | June 18, 2026 | TechCrunch

Amazon Web Services is in talks to sell its homegrown Trainium AI chips to third-party companies for use in their own data centers — a move that would directly challenge Nvidia's dominance in the AI hardware market. AWS AI chief Peter DeSantis confirmed the plans to Bloomberg, though he declined to name potential buyers.

The push comes from CEO Andy Jassy's April shareholder letter, in which he said that if AWS's chips business were standalone, its annual run rate would be approximately $50 billion. "There's so much demand for our chips that it's quite possible we'll sell racks of them to third parties in the future," Jassy wrote.

AWS has historically resisted selling chips externally because the real revenue comes from the "waterfall effect" — charging for AI tokens, storage, security, networking, and monitoring services around the chips. But the Trainium business has grown so large that a $50 billion competitor wouldn't tank Nvidia's $326 billion revenue run rate; it would be akin to adding another Intel-sized player overnight.

**Key Takeaways:**
- AWS Trainium chips could become available to third-party data centers
- standalone chip business would be ~$50B annual run rate
- Biggest challenge to Nvidia's AI chip dominance so far
- Amazon benefits from both direct sales and cloud service revenue
- Signals the AI hardware market is ripe for competition beyond Nvidia

### AI Inference Startup Baseten Raises $1.5B at $13B Valuation

![Baseten inference](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/baseten-inference.jpg?resize=1200,800)

**Funding** | June 18, 2026 | TechCrunch

AI inference startup Baseten is close to finalizing a $1.5 billion funding round at a $13 billion valuation, the Wall Street Journal reports. The raise comes just five months after Baseten secured $300 million at a $5 billion valuation — a 160% increase in less than half a year.

The deal is reportedly a "split-priced round," where some investors come in at $13 billion and others at $11 billion, a tactic startups use to boost headline valuations. The round is co-led by Spark Capital, Sands Capital, Altimeter Capital, and Wellington Management.

Launched in 2019, Baseten sits in the "inference gold rush" — the layer that handles what happens after a user submits a prompt. Baseten routes requests to the best-for-task model, steering users toward capable but less expensive open source alternatives to control costs.

**Key Takeaways:**
- $1.5B round at $13B valuation, up 160% from 5 months ago
- Split-priced round: some investors at $13B, others at $11B
- Co-led by Spark Capital, Sands Capital, Altimeter, Wellington
- Part of the "inference gold rush" as VCs pour money into the inference layer
- Routing to cheaper open source models is the core cost-control strategy

### Mukesh Ambani Brings AI to 500 Million Indians

![Reliance Jio](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/reliance-jio-ai.jpg?resize=1200,800)

**Industry** | June 19, 2026 | TechCrunch

Billionaire Mukesh Ambani is positioning Reliance Industries as India's national AI champion, rolling out AI services across phone calls, mobile apps, and connected homes. At Reliance's annual shareholder meeting, the company announced three major products: Jio Call Agent (an AI assistant that joins phone calls to transcribe, summarize, and perform tasks), an AI-powered MyJio app (handling eSIM activation, roaming plans via natural language), and TeleFrame (a home display using AI agents to surface weather, schedules, and reminders).

Jio Call Agent can be activated by saying "Hey Jio" and is expected to launch later this year for Jio's 500+ million users. By embedding AI directly into the telecom network rather than offering it as a standalone app, Reliance bets AI assistance becomes a native feature of phone calls — reducing reliance on third-party apps.

"India should not be a mere consumer of AI created elsewhere. It must become a creator, adopter, and a global leader in AI," said Ambani, 69.

**Key Takeaways:**
- Jio Call Agent joins and summarizes phone calls for 500M+ users
- MyJio app handles tasks via natural language
- TeleFrame is an AI home display for proactive information
- Embedded in telecom network, not a standalone app — distribution advantage
- Part of India's push for sovereign AI capabilities

### FERC Fast-Tracks AI Data Centers to the Power Grid

![Data center power](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/data-center-grid-1152x648.jpg)

**Policy** | June 18, 2026 | TechCrunch

The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) has ordered grid operators to fast-track interconnection requests from data centers and other large electricity users. Under the orders, six major grid operators must ensure data centers can connect to the transmission system "in a timely and orderly manner." Data centers will bear the interconnection costs.

The commission also directed grid operators to consider "alternative transmission technologies" — potentially including solid-state transformers or superconducting transmission lines — and to be more accommodating to behind-the-meter power for data centers.

However, the order doesn't address the fundamental problem: a shortage of generating capacity. Grid connections have been slow partly because new power plants themselves can't get connected. At the end of 2023, grid connection requests for power plants exceeded the total capacity of the existing fleet.

**Key Takeaways:**
- FERC ordered six grid operators to fast-track data center interconnections
- Data centers responsible for interconnection costs
- Encourages alternative transmission tech (solid-state transformers, superconducting lines)
- Does NOT address the underlying power generation shortage
- Grid connection requests already exceed total existing fleet capacity

### OpenAI Hires Transformer Co-Author Noam Shazeer Ahead of IPO

![Noam Shazeer](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/noam-shazeer.jpg?resize=1200,800)

**Industry** | June 18, 2026 | TechCrunch

OpenAI has hired Noam Shazeer, one of the most foundational minds in modern generative AI, away from Google DeepMind. Shazeer co-authored the seminal 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need" which introduced the Transformer architecture — the foundation of every major AI model today.

Shazeer had been at Google since 2000 (minus a three-year stint co-founding Character AI, which Google reacquired in a $2.7 billion deal). His departure from Google was announced Wednesday. He's also credited with early work on large language models during his first tenure at Google Brain.

The hire is one of several high-profile shufflings between top AI labs. OpenAI also brought on Dean Ball, former White House AI policy official who helped publish America's AI Action Plan, to lead a new "Strategic Futures" team focused on frontier AI policy — a clear signal that OpenAI is preparing for regulatory scrutiny ahead of its expected IPO.

**Key Takeaways:**
- Noam Shazeer co-authored "Attention Is All You Need" (Transformer paper)
- Left Google DeepMind where he was a Gemini co-lead
- Also brings in Dean Ball (ex-White House AI policy) for "Strategic Futures" team
- Hiring spree signals IPO preparation
- OpenAI shoring up both technical firepower and policy credentials simultaneously

### Snap Spins Off AI Video Team into Dotmo Due to Costs

![Snap AI video](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/snap-ai-video.jpg?resize=1200,800)

**Industry** | June 18, 2026 | TechCrunch

Snap is spinning off its generative AI video team into a separate company called Dotmo, focused on developing AI models for interactive gaming experiences. Snap cited the high costs of conducting such work internally as the primary reason for the spinoff.

While technically independent, Dotmo retains close ties to Snap. Snap provides a technology license, the founding team comes from Snap staff, and Snap CTO Bobby Murphy is lead investor with a significant personal stake. Snap receives a large equity stake in Dotmo in return.

This is Snap's second major spinoff this year — earlier in 2026, it spun off Specs to focus on smart glasses. Snap also underwent layoffs earlier this year, cutting roughly 1,000 jobs.

**Key Takeaways:**
- AI video team spun off into Dotmo to focus on interactive gaming
- High internal costs drove the decision
- Snap CTO Bobby Murphy is lead investor
- Snap gets large equity stake in return
- Second Snap spinoff this year after Specs (smart glasses)

### General Intuition Raises $300M at $2B Valuation for Embodied AI

![General Intuition](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/general-intuition.jpg?resize=1200,800)

**Funding** | June 18, 2026 | TechCrunch

General Intuition, a New York startup building foundation models that teach AI agents to navigate space and time, is in talks to raise $300 million at a $2 billion valuation. The raise comes just eight months after spinning out of Medal (a gaming clip-sharing platform) with a $134 million seed round.

The startup trains embodied AI and world models using Medal's dataset of 2 billion videos per year from 10 million monthly active users. The pitch: first-person gameplay footage teaches machines deep spatial-temporal reasoning — how to perceive, anticipate, and interact in real time in simulation.

Backers reportedly include Jeff Bezos, Eric Schmidt, Khosla Ventures, and General Catalyst. OpenAI had previously attempted to acquire Medal, underscoring the value of this unique dataset.

- Trained on 2B gameplay videos/year from 10M users via Medal dataset

### Trump Administration Helps xAI Fight Pollution Lawsuit, Citing Military Need for Grok

![xAI data center](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/xai-colossus-1152x648.jpg)

**Policy** | June 18, 2026 | Ars Technica

The Trump administration is intervening on behalf of Elon Musk's xAI to block a Clean Air Act lawsuit filed by the NAACP. The lawsuit alleges xAI violated environmental law by operating up to 57 unpermitted gas turbines at its Colossus data center in Southaven, Mississippi, causing health concerns and noise complaints.

The Department of Justice argued the lawsuit "threatens artificial-intelligence innovation, plus the energy needed to power it," and claimed Grok provides "critical support for the Department of War's military operations." A Pentagon official declared that Grok Gov Model helped deploy over 2,000 munitions to 2,000 targets within 96 hours during Operation Epic Fury in Iran.

The Southern Environmental Law Center, representing the NAACP, responded that the US is arguing "xAI should be allowed to break the law solely because the Trump administration says so."

**Key Takeaways:**
- DOJ trying to dismiss NAACP Clean Air Act lawsuit against xAI
- Up to 57 unpermitted gas turbines at Mississippi data center
- Government claims Grok is critical for military operations
- Pentagon says Grok helped deploy 2,000 munitions in 96 hours
- Raises questions about whether AI companies get special legal treatment

## Why This Matters Today

The AI industry is entering a phase where the constraints are no longer just technical — they're infrastructural, geopolitical, and almost absurd.

Amazon's move to sell Trainium chips shows that even Nvidia's stranglehold on AI hardware has an expiration date. When the second-largest cloud provider starts selling its own silicon, the competitive landscape shifts permanently. Combined with Baseten's meteoric $13B valuation in the inference layer, it's clear the AI money isn't just in training anymore — it's in the entire stack.

Meanwhile, the physical realities of AI are catching up. FERC's fast-track order for data center grid connections is a Band-Aid on a bullet wound: we're building AI infrastructure faster than we can power it. And the xAI pollution lawsuit — where the government argues that breaking environmental laws is acceptable because the military needs Grok — shows how "national security" is becoming a wildcard that could override any regulation.

India's push, led by Ambani, to embed AI into 500 million phone calls isn't just ambition — it's a statement that the next billion AI users won't be English-speaking Americans. The geopolitical map of AI is being redrawn, and it's not centered on Silicon Valley anymore.

And through it all, the talent wars intensify. Shazeer jumping from Google to OpenAI isn't just a hire — it's a signal that the IPO race has turned into an arms race for the people who literally wrote the book on Transformers.

The AI industry isn't slowing down. It's just running into walls faster.
