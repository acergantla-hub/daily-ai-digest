### Europe Pushes Back on Washington's Chip War

**Hardware** | June 25, 2026 | TechCrunch

European leaders and companies are increasingly pushing back against US-led semiconductor export restrictions targeting China. ASML's CEO noted that China can only purchase older-generation DUV lithography systems shipped a decade ago, questioning the effectiveness of current controls. Meanwhile, the proposed MATCH Act would further tighten restrictions, drawing criticism from European allies who view the measures as extraterritorial overreach that harms their own semiconductor equipment industry.

**Key Takeaways:**
- Europe is mounting resistance to US semiconductor export controls on China
- ASML CEO highlights that restrictions only push China toward older technology

- Transatlantic tensions grow over the economic impact of chip war policies
